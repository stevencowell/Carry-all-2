Week 8: Assembly, Squareness & Dry Fitting Techniques

Introduction

This is a critical week in your Carry-all project — it’s time to begin assembling the main body. That means dry fitting parts, checking alignment, and clamping things in place before committing to glue. Get this step wrong, and even perfect joints won’t save your project.

Dry Fitting – Why Test Before You Glue?

Dry fitting means assembling parts without glue to:

  • Check for fit and alignment
  • Confirm all pieces are present and oriented correctly
  • Test how clamps will be positioned
  • Identify where adjustments are needed before glue goes everywhere

Pro Tip: Mark lightly with pencil where clamps go and which face is outward to avoid assembly mistakes.

Checking for Squareness

Once parts are dry fitted or clamped, you need to check that everything is square:

  • Use a try square or measure diagonals corner-to-corner
  • If diagonals are equal, the box is square
  • Even small misalignments can cause problems later

Gluing & Clamping in Stages

Don’t glue everything at once. Instead:

  • Glue one or two sides at a time
  • Clamp firmly and wipe away excess glue
  • Allow each stage to dry fully before moving on

Clamping Tips

  • Use scrap timber pads between clamps and project to avoid dents
  • Don’t overtighten — firm pressure is enough
  • Place clamps opposite each other and spaced evenly to prevent twisting

Discussion Starters

  • Why is dry fitting essential before final assembly?
  • Have you ever glued something up too fast and regretted it?
  • What’s your method for checking if a box is square?
