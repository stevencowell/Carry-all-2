Week 5: Timber Careers & Technology – Where Woodworking Can Take You

Introduction

You’ve learned a lot of hands-on skills, but did you know they can lead to real careers? This week, we’ll look at different timber industry career paths — from framing houses to making kitchens — and how technology like CAD/CAM is transforming the way work gets done.

Key Timber Industry Careers

  1. Carpenter
    Works on-site building and assembling structures: framing, roofing, fitting fixtures; uses nail guns, levels, saws, layout gear.
  2. Joiner
    Makes timber components (windows, stairs) in a workshop; focuses on precision and fittings; often uses CAD software and CNC machines.
  3. Cabinetmaker
    Builds fine furniture, cabinetry, custom kitchen/bath fittings; combines traditional hand tools with advanced machines; known for high levels of finishing.
  4. Wood Machinist
    Operates large woodworking machines in factories or workshops; prepares timber to exact specifications; requires safety skills and understanding of G-code.

From Design to Machine: CAD/CAM Workflow

Modern tradespeople use both hands-on and digital tools in a workflow:

  • CAD (Computer-Aided Design): draw models in 2D/3D software.
  • CAM (Computer-Aided Manufacturing): convert models into machine instructions.
  • CNC Operation: use machines to cut parts precisely and repeatably.

Future-Focused Materials: CLT & Glulam

Engineered timbers are replacing steel and concrete in some builds:

  • CLT (Cross Laminated Timber): stacked layers for strength, used in tall buildings.
  • Glulam (Glued Laminated Timber): long beams made from finger-jointed boards.
