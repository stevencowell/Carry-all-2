Week 7: Fasteners, Adhesives & Holding Workpieces Securely

Introduction

As your Carry-all begins to take shape, the focus shifts to how parts are held together—both temporarily while you work and permanently once you glue or fasten.

Adhesives: The Invisible Strength

In modern woodworking, PVA (polyvinyl acetate) glue is most commonly used. It's strong, non-toxic, and dries clear. For a strong glue joint:

  • Surfaces must fit snugly with no large gaps
  • Apply an even coat of glue
  • Clamp the joint tightly and let it cure undisturbed

Mechanical Fasteners: Screws & Nails

Screws are excellent for strengthening joins and allowing removal if needed. Nails are quicker but weaker and often used temporarily.

Holding Work Securely: Clamps, Vices & Jigs

Your hands are not clamps. Proper holding ensures accuracy, safety, and cleaner cuts. Common tools include:

  • G-clamps/F-clamps for adjustable pressure
  • Bench vice for sawing or chiselling
  • Bench hook essential for handsawing
  • Jigs for repetitive or complex clamping

Discussion Starters
